<h2>About the Workshop</h2>
<p>Date: 27/28 [TBC] April 2025 @ ICLR</p>
<p>Fundamental laws of Nature, Standard Model of Physics, and the most applied part of it, quantum mechanics, are well established. Theoretically, the dynamics of anything starting from a hydrogen atom and all the way to Earth's climate follow those equations. The problem is complexity <a href="https://royalsocietypublishing.org/doi/10.1098/rspa.1929.0094" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">Dirac 1929</a>. An exact computation of a modest system containing 100 atoms is still beyond the capability of modern computers.</p>
<p>Some of the greatest scientific achievements resulted from breakthroughs in scale transitions: renormalization, density functional theory, Higgs boson, multiscale models for complex chemical systems, climate modeling, protein folding. Those achievements are highly regarded because they are impactful – but also unique and can't be readily applied to different systems.</p>
<p>Encouraged by the recent successes, this workshop aims to enable the development of <em>universal</em> AI methods that would be able to find efficient and accurate approximations, and use them for some of the most pressing and high-impact scientific problems that have computational complexity as the limiting factor to an in silico solution, such as:</p>
<ul>
  <li>High-temperature superconductivity</li>
  <li>Fusion power</li>
  <li>Weather prediction</li>
  <li>Catalysts</li>
  <li>Brain and consciousness</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>If we solve scale transition, we solve science.</strong></p>
